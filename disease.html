Clinical Condition Guide

A comprehensive overview of the otoscopic pathologies recognized by our proprietary AI model.

✅ Normal Anatomy

The tympanic membrane appears pearly-gray and translucent. The "Cone of Light" reflex is clearly visible, and no signs of inflammation or fluid are present. Indicates a healthy middle ear environment.

🔴 Acute Otitis Media (AOM)

A rapid onset middle ear infection. Characterized by a severely bulging, red (erythematous) eardrum, often accompanied by intense pain, fever, and purulent fluid buildup behind the membrane.

🟣 Chronic Otitis Media (COM)

A persistent, long-standing infection that may lead to permanent destructive changes in the ear architecture, such as a perforated eardrum or continuous purulent discharge (otorrhea).

🟠 Cerumen Impaction

A dense build-up of earwax that completely obstructs the ear canal. This prevents visual assessment of the eardrum and requires safe clinical removal prior to proper diagnostic evaluation.

🔵 Myringosclerosis

Hardened calcium deposits within the tympanic membrane, typically arising after recurrent episodes of chronic inflammation. Presents as distinct, opaque, chalky-white plaques on the drum surface.
